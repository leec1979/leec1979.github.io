This is Biscuit.

![Biscuit](/images/Biscuit.jpg "Biscuit")

You're probably looking at that picture and thinking how cute he looks. How he seems like such a good, well-behaved cat. Yes...and no.

Biscuit is my inspiration for a number of projects. My aim is to solve as many Biscuit related problems as possible using a [Raspberry Pi](https://www.raspberrypi.org).

The first problem is not actually an issue now we've moved to the US (Yes, Biscuit moved with me from the UK). In the UK Biscuit had a cat flap and could come and go as he pleased. This was great for Biscuit's freedom and sense of adventure. It was not so great from the "Oh look, a dead mouse or bird." perspective.

As the number of dead (and sometimes living) wildlife being brought into the house grew I started thinking about how to keep Biscuit out of the house when he had a victim in his mouth. I eventually settled on the idea of a cat flap with facial recognition. If I could teach the cat flap to recognise Biscuit's face, it should then be able to determine whether or not he had a dead something in his mouth.

Enter the Raspberry Pi plus camera module.
